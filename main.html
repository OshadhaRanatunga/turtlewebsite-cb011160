Learn about turtles

Welcome to our Sri Lankan turtle conservation website! Sri Lanka is a vital nesting ground for five species of sea turtles, including the Olive Ridley, Green Turtle, Leatherback, Hawksbill, and Loggerhead. Sadly, these gentle creatures are endangered due to human activities such as poaching, pollution, and habitat destruction. Our organization is dedicated to protecting and conserving these majestic creatures by providing a safe environment for them to lay their eggs and hatch their young. We also aim to educate the public about the importance of sea turtle conservation and promote sustainable tourism. Join us in our mission to protect these wonderful creatures and ensure their survival for generations to come.

breeding is a critical component of efforts to conserve and protect these magnificent creatures. Breeding programs aim to increase the population of turtles by facilitating successful reproduction and ensuring the survival of hatchlings. There are different breeding programs in place, including in-situ and ex-situ breeding. In-situ breeding occurs in the natural habitats of turtles, while ex-situ breeding happens in controlled environments, such as turtle hatcheries and captive breeding facilities. In-situ breeding programs aim to preserve and protect turtle habitats and nesting areas, ensuring that conditions are conducive to successful breeding. The programs focus on reducing the impacts of human activity on turtle habitats, which often include pollution, habitat destruction, and poaching. By preserving and protecting these habitats, the turtles are given the best possible chance of breeding successfully and increasing their populations. Ex-situ breeding programs, on the other hand, involve the collection of turtle eggs or adult turtles for breeding in captivity. These programs are typically established when the wild populations of turtles are at risk of extinction or are already critically endangered. In these cases, the breeding programs serve as a last resort to save the species from extinction. The facilities provide a controlled environment for the turtles, which allows for careful monitoring and management of breeding activity, as well as the hatching and rearing of hatchlings. In summary, turtle breeding programs are essential in ensuring the survival and growth of turtle populations. These programs require a significant amount of resources and expertise to be successful. However, they have the potential to make a significant impact in conserving these magnificent creatures for generations to come.
